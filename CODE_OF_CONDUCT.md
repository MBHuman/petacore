# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inspiring, and harassment-free community for all, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, or socioeconomic status.

We expect all members of the PetaCore community to uphold these values and create an environment where everyone feels respected, valued, and able to contribute their best work.

## Expected Behavior

All community members are expected to:

### Be Respectful
- Treat all community members with kindness and respect
- Listen actively and try to understand different perspectives
- Give credit to others for their contributions
- Be open to receiving feedback gracefully

### Be Inclusive
- Welcome newcomers and help them get started
- Use inclusive language that doesn't assume or exclude
- Create space for diverse voices and experiences
- Make effort to accommodate different needs and abilities

### Be Constructive
- Focus on the work, not on individuals
- Provide helpful, actionable feedback
- Acknowledge good ideas and implementations
- Help others improve and learn

### Be Professional
- Keep discussions focused on the project
- Respect the time and effort of others
- Be responsive and reliable in your commitments
- Address conflicts privately and diplomatically

### Be Transparent
- Be honest about your capabilities and limitations
- Acknowledge mistakes and work to correct them
- Explain your decisions and reasoning
- Ask questions when something is unclear

## Unacceptable Behavior

We do not tolerate behavior that:

### Harassment
- Deliberate intimidation or threats
- Sustained, targeted attacks or insults
- Unwelcome romantic or sexual advances
- Offensive comments related to protected characteristics
- Doxxing or publishing others' private information

### Discrimination
- Exclusion based on protected characteristics
- Stereotyping or generalizing about groups
- Differential treatment or opportunities
- Creating an unwelcoming environment

### Hostile Communication
- Aggressive or demeaning language directed at individuals
- Trolling or deliberately provocative behavior
- Spam or off-topic disruption
- Abusive comments in any forum (code reviews, issues, discussions)

### Abuse of Power
- Using moderator status to suppress opposing views
- Taking advantage of your position for personal gain
- Excluding others unfairly from opportunities
- Making decisions unilaterally on communal matters without input

## Reporting Guidelines

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to the Code of Conduct team.

### How to Report

**For immediate concerns:**
Contact the project maintainers directly:
- Open a confidential GitHub issue (if available)
- Email: conduct@petacore-project.org (if applicable)
- Private message a maintainer

**Include in your report:**
1. Your name and contact information
2. Names/usernames of individuals involved
3. Description of the incident
4. Date and location (online venue, chat channel, etc.)
5. Any relevant context or witnesses
6. What happened and how you were affected
7. Previous incidents (if applicable)

### Confidentiality

All reports will be:
- Handled confidentially
- Investigated thoroughly and promptly
- Treated with appropriate seriousness
- Protected from retaliation

**Names will not be disclosed** to the reported person without consent, except:
- When necessary to investigate or enforce consequences
- When legally required
- To protect safety

## Enforcement

### Investigation Process

1. **Assessment** - Determine if the report describes a violation
2. **Investigation** - Gather information from relevant parties
3. **Discussion** - Involved parties given opportunity to respond
4. **Decision** - Determine whether violation occurred
5. **Consequence** - Apply appropriate action if needed

### Consequences

Violations may result in:

**First Offense:**
- Warning and guidance about expectations
- Mandatory reflection or learning resources
- Temporary restriction from certain channels

**Repeated Violations:**
- Temporary suspension from community spaces
- Public or private apology (as appropriate)
- Mandatory mediation or coaching

**Severe or Persistent Violations:**
- Permanent ban from the community
- Removal of contributions (in extreme cases)
- Report to relevant authorities (if applicable)

**Note:** Severity is considered - a single severe incident may warrant immediate stronger action.

### Appeals

Decisions can be appealed to a Code of Conduct review committee within 14 days of notification.

## Community Standards by Space

### In Code (Pull Requests, Issues)

- Express disagreement respectfully
- Focus on code, not the coder
- Acknowledge good work and learning
- Help reviewer understand your perspective
- Don't take criticism personally

**Example:**
```
✅ Good: "I see your concern about performance. Have you considered using a hash map instead for O(1) lookups?"

❌ Bad: "This implementation is obviously inefficient. Anyone should know better."
```

### In Discussions & Chat

- Stay on topic
- Use threads to keep conversations organized
- Respect people's time zones
- Don't spam or flood channels
- Assume good intent

**Example:**
```
✅ Good: "Great question! That's a common confusion. Here's an explanation..."

❌ Bad: "That's a stupid question."
```

### In Documentation & Tutorials

- Use accessible language
- Acknowledge edge cases
- Welcome questions and corrections
- Appreciate contributions
- Provide constructive feedback

## FAQ

**Q: Is this Code of Conduct enforced?**
A: Yes, completely. Behavioral standards are taken seriously and enforced consistently.

**Q: What if I made a mistake?**
A: It's okay. We all make mistakes. The key is acknowledging it, apologizing (if appropriate), and doing better. We're here to help people learn and grow.

**Q: What about freedom of speech?**
A: The Code of Conduct applies to the PetaCore community spaces. Outside of PetaCore discussions, what you do is your business. But in our community, we prioritize making everyone welcome.

**Q: Who enforces this?**
A: The Code of Conduct is enforced by project maintainers with input from the community. For serious matters, a Code of Conduct committee may be formed.

**Q: Can someone appeal a decision?**
A: Yes. All enforcement decisions can be appealed within 14 days through the formal appeals process.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:
- GitHub repositories (issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Official communication channels (email, chat)
- Events (virtual or in-person)
- Related community spaces

It applies to everyone - contributors, maintainers, sponsors, and community members.
